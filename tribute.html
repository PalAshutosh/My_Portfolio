Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 – 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator at DRDO and ISRO, contributing to India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He was known as the Missile Man of India for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He also played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

  • Bharat Ratna in 1997 by the Government of India
  • Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration in 1997
  • Veer Savarkar Award in 1998
  • SASTRA Ramanujan Prize in 2000 by SASTRA University
